# Doctrine — Disability Employment + Assistive AI

**Claimed 2026-06-24.** This is a mission-level commitment, not a feature.

## What we believe

People with disabilities have been systematically locked out of meaningful, well-paid work — not because they can't contribute, but because the tools weren't built for them. Whitelabel.dev's commitment: **use humanoid robots + AI assist to remove the access barriers and create real, paid employment opportunities.**

## What "real employment" means here

- **Paid** at market rates for the work, not stipends. Charity supports the platform; the workers are workers, not beneficiaries.
- **Meaningful** — actual roles inside the ecosystem (and reseller agencies' clients) that move work forward.
- **Sustained** — full-time tracks where they exist, not gig-bursts.
- **With the right scaffolding** — humanoid robot for motor-skill constraints, AI assist for cognitive/sensory, adaptive UI for everyone.

## Non-negotiables when designing anything in the ecosystem

1. **Track 2 is not a wrapper around Track 1.** Accessible UI for the median user is necessary but not sufficient. The disabled-worker surface is the primary lens. If a design helps the median and degrades the disabled-worker experience, the design is wrong.

2. **The assist is the worker's tool, not surveillance of them.** AI that watches and reports is the wrong product. AI that the worker controls and benefits from is the right one.

3. **Funding flows through `whitelabel-charity`; work happens in `whitelabel-accessibility` + `whitelabel-robotics` + every surface that touches a worker.**

4. **Reseller-channel parity.** Agencies that resell whitelabel.dev should be able to hire disabled workers through the platform with the same accessibility scaffolding included by default. This is not an upsell.

5. **Government-funded path is real and the right path.** This is not a "do good on the side" — it's a path to revenue. See [`whitelabel-accessibility/funding/government-pathways.md`](https://github.com/whitelabel-dev/whitelabel-accessibility) for the channel-by-channel plan (VR vendor approval, SBIR, AbilityOne, Section 503, NIDILRR, Medicaid HCBS, Ticket to Work).
